BALANCE DRAFT
Current core rules:
+ Day length: 60s
+ Tool action delay: 1.5s per action
+ Basic crop action cost: till + plant + water + harvest = 4 actions = 6s, before walking/refilling
+ Bucket capacity: 10 water
+ Water rule: watered tiles stay watered until harvest or new day
+ Day reset: bucket refills, watered tiles dry
+ Crop persistence: planted crops linger across days if not harvested
+ Quota check: based on today's shipped value, not wallet gold
+ Starting quota: 40g
+ Quota increase: +30g per day
+ Starting gold: 0g
+ Starter seeds: 10 turnip seeds
+ Unsold harvested inventory auto-sells at day end so effort counts
+ Field crops are never auto-harvested
+ Planted crops persist across days until manually harvested
+ Mature crops stay mature until harvested
+ Future rule: manual bin shipping can give a small bonus

Balance implications:
+ Sell value is gross quota progress, not pure profit once seed costs exist
+ Profit should be sellValue - seedCost
+ High sell value crops help quota even if seed cost is high
+ Slow crops are less risky than they look because they can finish tomorrow, but they still tie up tile space and delay today's quota
+ Fast crops should be useful rescue crops, not the best long-term profit
+ Expensive/slow crops should reward early planning and surplus gold
+ Water is currently equal across crops because every crop needs only one watering
+ If crops later need multiple watering, slow crops become much riskier

ASSET NOTES
Kenney.nl: Often called the "Gold Standard" of free assets. Everything is CC0, meaning you can use it for anything without even giving credit. Look for his "Pixel" or "RPG" packs.
Itch.io (Free Assets): This is a goldmine. Thousands of indie artists post free packs here. You can filter specifically for "Top-down" and "Pixel Art."
OpenGameArt.org: An older but massive community-driven site. You can find everything from character sprite sheets to environment tilesets.
CraftPix (Freebies Section): They have high-quality "Top-down" tilesets (grass, paths, towns) that would work perfectly for a Firebase multiplayer map.
